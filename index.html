STAR Story 4

PMI Principle 9 - Recognize, Evaluate, and Respond to System Interactions

Situation

During the semester, changes in one class or area of life often affected other responsibilities. For example, heavy workloads from multiple classes landed in the same week, which created strain.

Task

I needed to understand how these interacting factors influenced my ability to complete project management assignments effectively.

Action

I adjusted my workload by planning ahead and reorganizing tasks. When I saw that a demanding week was coming up, I started assignments earlier or split them into smaller parts throughout the week. I monitored how changes in my schedule affected my overall progress.

Result

I finished my assignments without falling behind and developed a better sense of how different responsibilities affect each other. This helped me understand the systems-thinking mindset that project managers use in real organizations.
